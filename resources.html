About Anxiety

Anxiety is a recognized mental health disorder that can give the affected persons constant feelings of worry, irrational fears and many other detrimental feelings of phobias. It is typically classified as being clinical when the fears and worries are strong enough and constant enough that they interfere with daily life making it difficult or even impossible to live a complete life. Some common forms of anxiety are Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD), Panic disorders, Social anxiety, Separation Anxiety, various phobias, Obsessive-compulsive Disorder, Trauma related disorders and many others.

    • About Depression

    Depression is a mental health condition that can affect people of any age, it is characterized by consistently depressed moods and loss of interest in normally enjoyable or rewarding activities. This can cause severe impairment in one’s daily life leading to other detrimental effects such as loss of a job, poor performance in school, loss of social and romantic relationships and other negative things that only worsen the depression overall.

  • National Institute of Mental Health

    • About PTSD

    Posttraumatic Stress Disorder is a psychiatric disorder that is characterized by intense emotional feelings, flashbacks, nightmares and an inability to control one’s thoughts or emotions due to witnessing an extremely stressful or traumatic event. It is common in military veterans as the horrors of war can often spark PTSD in an individual, but PTSD is not just limited to those who have served in military conflicts.

    • About Abuse

    About Abuse: Victims of abuse might not have a disorder or condition, but abuse can lead downs roads of depression, PTSD or anxiety and the best way to overcome the aftereffects of being abused is usually to openly deal with it, such as with a therapist. Abuse can take many forms including but not limited to; sexual abuse, domestic violence, drug or alcohol abuse, emotional abuse or psychological abuse.
